SUNDAY NIGHT: COSMIC PROG-SORCERY - A JOURNEY TO THE DARK SIDE OF THE MOON (LITERALLY, A ROCK IS COMING LOOSE)

Headliner: The Arcane Floyd

The architects of auditory awe, the sultans of synaptic soundscapes. This isn't a performance; it's a three-hour collective astral projection. Lie on the "Psychic Repose" grass field (a.k.a. the muddy ditch they won't let us charge extra for), and let the synesthesia take hold.

  • Full album performance of The Wizard's Side of the Moon: From the heartbeats and manic laughter in "Speak to Me (Oh Great Spirit)" to the soul-crushing loneliness of "Brain Damage (From Unsanctioned Scrying)."
  • Eclipse of the Sorcerer: A 23-minute guitar solo played on a lute strung with captured sunbeams and wailing phantoms. May cause temporary blindness and profound philosophical regret.
  • The grand finale: Another Scribe in the Wall, Parts 1, 2, and 3: A blistering indictment of magical academia, standardized familiar testing, and the soul-grinding monotony of transcribing spellbooks. Features a chorus of one thousand illusory children chanting "We don't need no incantation / We don't need no thought control" before a giant, metaphorical wall of fire consumes the stage. FRIDAY NIGHT: THE BONE-RATTLING REVELRY - MOSH MAGIC and MINOR TEARS IN THE VEIL

Headliner: The Rolling Bones

These skeletal stalwarts of stadium rock-n-roll necromancy are back from the dead (again) and angrier than a basilisk with a stone complex. Their setlist is a grimoire of gritty anthems that will have you headbanging so hard your pointy hat will become a lethal projectile.

  • Sympathy for the Wizard: A haunting ballad about the existential crisis of having ultimate power but still having to unclog your own temporal sinkhole.
  • Wizard's Tongue: Not for the easily offended or literally cursed. A rollicking tune about gossip that spreads faster than a plague sprite.
  • Paint It, Black (with Arcane Ink): Their iconic finale, where the entire Moor is plunged into an abyssal darkness, illuminated only by violently swirling, sentient tattoos that leak from the stage. The Mosh Pit for this number is less a dance and more a ritualized combat sport.
